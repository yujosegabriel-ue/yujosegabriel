My Island Roots:
Gab's Journey of Growth

A Story of Growth and Identity

Jose Gabriel Yu | ITAD

Childhood Years

I grew up in the quiet and beautiful province of Masbate, where life moved slowly and peacefully.1 My childhood was filled with long days under the sun and the fresh smell of grass from the open fields.2 I spent countless afternoons playing traditional Filipino games with school kids who became like siblings to me.3 We played bente uno until our legs grew tired and our laughter echoed across the street.4 Agawang base became our favorite pastime, especially during warm summer evenings.5 I also collected pogs, which felt like small treasures that made my young heart excited.6 Each new pog made me feel like I discovered gold.7 I loved climbing trees, especially mango trees that stood tall near our house.8 Reaching the highest branch always felt like winning a secret challenge against nature.9 Sometimes we would sit on the branches and tell stories while feeling the wind brush against our faces.10 Life in Masbate felt simple yet magical in its own way.11 I never had many toys, but the outdoors became my playground.12 I learned creativity from making games out of anything I could find.13 My family taught me humility and kindness at a very early age.14 They also taught me to appreciate small things in life.15 Growing up surrounded by nature made me feel connected to the world.16 Every day felt like a new adventure waiting for me outside our doorstep.17 My childhood shaped who I am today in ways I will always be grateful for.18 Those moments of innocence and fun remain some of my greatest memories.19 Masbate will forever hold the roots of my earliest and happiest days.20

Teenage Years

My high school years were a time of discovery and building new perspectives.1 I started taking my interest in technology more seriously during this period.2 Life in the province remained steady, but my mind was often elsewhere, dreaming of bigger things.3 I learned the value of friendship through the bonds I formed with my classmates.4 We spent hours talking about our futures while sitting by the bay.5 Those years taught me how to handle the pressure of academic life.6 I began to understand that hard work was the only way to reach my goals.7 Every challenge I faced made me more determined to succeed.8 I participated in school events that helped me build my confidence.9 The simple life in Masbate provided a grounded foundation as I grew up.10 I realized that my roots would always be a part of my identity.11 Independence started becoming more important to me as I got older.12 I spent my free time exploring the island’s hidden gems with my friends.13 The sunsets in Masbate served as a daily reminder of nature's beauty.14 I learned that patience is vital when waiting for big opportunities.15 Family remained my strongest support system throughout these changing years.16 I began to envision a life where I could blend my roots with modern technology.17 Self-discipline became a habit I tried to master every single day.18 My teenage years were the bridge between my childhood and the man I was becoming.19 They prepared me for the bigger world that was waiting just beyond the sea.20

College Years

My college years at UE marked one of the most transformative stages of my life.1 I entered school with a strong determination to build the foundation for my future career.2 Everything felt new, exciting, and a little overwhelming at first.3 I pushed myself academically to achieve high grades.4 Each semester challenged me to become more disciplined.5 I made new friends who eventually became like family.6 We shared struggles, triumphs, and unforgettable memories.7 Group projects taught me leadership and teamwork.8 I joined organizations that helped me grow not only academically but socially.9 College exposed me to real-life responsibilities that I had never experienced before.10 One of the biggest challenges I faced was dealing with financial problems.11 I learned how to budget every allowance and prioritize needs over wants.12 Those struggles taught me strength and independence.13 Despite the difficulties, I didn’t let go of my goals.14 I studied late at night just to keep my grades up.15 Every small achievement reminded me that hard work pays off.16 I embraced every opportunity to improve myself.17 College shaped me into a stronger and more resilient person.18 It prepared me for the real world and the career path I wanted to pursue.19 In the end, college became a journey of growth, friendship, challenges, and victories.20
